# FE Environmental — Official NCEES Exam Specification (relevance source of truth)

Use this to confirm **every question maps to a real exam topic** before it ships. Source:
NCEES *Fundamentals of Engineering (FE) Environmental CBT Exam Specifications* — effective
beginning July 2020 (current for the 2026 exam). 110 questions, 6 hours, closed-book with the
electronic NCEES FE Reference Handbook. Exam uses **both SI and USCS**.
PDF: https://ncees.org/wp-content/uploads/FE-Environmental-CBT-specs.pdf

The two biggest areas (12–18 Q each) are **Fluid Mechanics & Hydraulics** and **Water & Wastewater**;
**Surface Water/Hydrology** (9–14) and **Water & Wastewater** carry the most water-resources weight.

## Subtopics for each knowledge area
1. **Mathematics** — A. analytic geometry/trig · B. algebraic equations/roots · C. calculus · D. numerical methods
2. **Probability & Statistics** — A. central tendency/dispersion · B. distributions · C. estimation/CIs · D. regression/curve fitting · E. hypothesis testing
3. **Ethics & Professional Practice** — A. codes of ethics · B. public health/safety/welfare · C. codes/standards/regs (CWA, CAA, RCRA, CERCLA, SDWA, NEPA, OSHA) · D. engineer's role in society
4. **Engineering Economics** — A. time value of money · B. cost types · C. economic analyses · D. project selection
5. **Fundamental Principles** — A. population projections & demand · B. reactors · C. materials science
6. **Environmental Chemistry** — A. stoichiometry/reactions · B. kinetics · C. organic chemistry · D. multimedia equilibrium partitioning
7. **Health Hazards & Risk Assessment** — A. dose-response toxicity · B. exposure routes/pathways · C. occupational health
8. **Fluid Mechanics & Hydraulics** — A. fluid statics · B. closed conduits (Darcy-Weisbach, Hazen-Williams, Moody) · C. open channel (Manning, super/subcritical, culverts, hydraulic elements) · D. pumps · E. flow measurement (weirs, orifices, flumes) · F. blowers · G. fluid dynamics (Bernoulli, laminar/turbulent, continuity) · H. steady/unsteady flow
9. **Thermodynamics** — A. thermo laws · B. energy/heat/work · C. ideal gases
10. **Surface Water Resources & Hydrology** —
    - **A. Runoff calculations** (land use/cover, time of concentration, **duration, intensity, frequency**, runoff control/management) ← Rational Method, SCS/NRCS curve number, IDF, flood frequency / return period
    - **B. Water storage sizing** (reservoir, detention & retention basins)
    - **C. Routing** (channel, reservoir)
    - **D. Water quality & modeling** (erosion, channel stability, stormwater quality, wetlands, Streeter-Phelps, eutrophication)
    - **E. Water budget** (evapotranspiration, precipitation, infiltration, soil moisture, storage)
11. **Groundwater, Soils & Sediments** — A. basic hydrogeology · B. groundwater flow (Darcy's law, specific capacity, velocity, gradient, transport) · C. drawdown (Dupuit, Jacob, Theis, Thiem) · D. remediation
12. **Water & Wastewater** — A. characteristics · B. mass balance/loading · C. physical processes · D. chemical processes · E. biological processes · F. sludge treatment · G. conservation & reuse
13. **Air Quality & Control** — A. ambient/indoor air quality · B. mass/energy balances · C. emissions · D. atmospheric modeling/meteorology · E. gas treatment · F. particle treatment · G. indoor air quality modeling
14. **Solid & Hazardous Waste** — A. mass/energy balances · B. solid waste management · C. disposal (landfills, leachate/gas) · D. hazardous waste compatibility · E. site characterization · F. hazardous/radioactive treatment & disposal
15. **Energy & Environment** — A. energy source concepts · B. environmental impact of energy

## The relevance rule (applied to every question, every build)
1. Each question must map to a **specific KA subtopic above** (e.g. "10.A — frequency"). If it maps to nothing, **cut it** — it doesn't matter for the exam.
2. Prefer **spread across the day's KA subtopics** over piling onto one. (E.g. a Surface Water set that is all 10.A runoff and ignores 10.B storage sizing / 10.E water budget is narrower than the exam.)
3. Honor the **SI + USCS** mix — the real exam uses both.
4. Relevance (is it on the exam?) is separate from fidelity (is the formula in the Handbook?). Both gates must pass; see CLAUDE.md.
